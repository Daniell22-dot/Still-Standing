Coping with Anxiety

Practical tools to find calm in the chaos

Understanding Anxiety

Anxiety is your body's natural response to stress. It's a feeling of fear or apprehension about what's to come. While it can feel overwhelming, remember that it is a wave that will pass. You are safe.

1. Box Breathing

A simple, powerful technique to reset your nervous system.

Let's try it together:

  • Breathe IN for 4 seconds
  • HOLD for 4 seconds
  • Breathe OUT for 4 seconds
  • HOLD for 4 seconds

2. The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

Use your senses to bring yourself back to the present moment.

  • Acknowledge 5 things you see around you. (A pen, a spot on the ceiling, anything in your surroundings.)
  • Acknowledge 4 things you can touch. (Your hair, a pillow, the ground under your feet.)
  • Acknowledge 3 things you hear. (Any external sound. If you can hear your belly rumbling that counts!)
  • Acknowledge 2 things you can smell. (Maybe you are in your office and smell pencil, or maybe you are in your bedroom and smell a pillow.)
  • Acknowledge 1 thing you can taste. (What does the inside of your mouth taste like? Gum, coffee, or the sandwich from lunch?)

3. Challenge Anxious Thoughts

Anxiety often lies to us. Ask yourself these questions when you feel spiraling:

  • Is this thought based on fact or feeling?
  • What is the evidence FOR this thought?
  • What is the evidence AGAINST this thought?
  • What would I tell a friend who had this thought?
