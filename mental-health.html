Mental Health is Health

Just like physical health, mental health exists on a spectrum. Everyone has mental health, just like everyone has physical health. Some days are better than others, and that's completely normal.

Common Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Mental health issues are a sign of weakness

Fact: Mental health conditions are medical conditions, not character flaws. They require treatment and support, just like diabetes or hypertension.

Myth: You can just "snap out of it"

Fact: Mental health conditions involve real changes in brain chemistry and functioning. They require professional support and treatment.

Myth: Therapy is only for "crazy" people

Fact: Therapy is for anyone who wants to improve their mental wellbeing. It's like a gym for your mind.

Understanding Common Conditions

Knowledge reduces stigma and increases understanding

Depression

More than just sadness: A persistent feeling of emptiness, loss of interest, and low energy.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent sad or "empty" mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in sleep and appetite
  • Difficulty concentrating
Get Help for Depression

Anxiety Disorders

Excessive worry: Persistent, excessive fear or worry in situations that are not threatening.

Common Symptoms:

  • Racing thoughts
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Sweating or trembling
  • Avoidance of certain situations
Coping with Anxiety

PTSD

After trauma: A mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a terrifying event.

Common Symptoms:

  • Flashbacks or nightmares
  • Severe anxiety
  • Unwanted memories
  • Emotional numbness
Trauma Support

Other Conditions

Various challenges: Including bipolar disorder, OCD, eating disorders, and more.

Important:

Only qualified professionals can diagnose mental health conditions. If you're struggling, seek professional evaluation.

Practical Coping Strategies

Tools you can use right now

Grounding Techniques (5-4-3-2-1 Method)

When feeling overwhelmed, name:

  1. 5 things you can see
  2. 4 things you can touch
  3. 3 things you can hear
  4. 2 things you can smell
  5. 1 thing you can taste

Journaling Prompts

Writing can help process emotions. Try these prompts:

  • "Right now, I'm feeling..."
  • "What I really need today is..."
  • "One small win I had today..."
  • "What would I tell a friend who felt this way?"

Movement Breaks

Physical activity releases endorphins. Try:

  • 5 minutes of stretching
  • A short walk around the block
  • Dancing to one favorite song
  • Gentle yoga or deep breathing

Calming Breath Exercise

Use this when you feel anxious or overwhelmed. Follow the circle's rhythm.

Breathe In

Instructions: Breathe in as the circle expands (4 seconds), hold (4 seconds), breathe out as it shrinks (6 seconds).

Emergency Services

999 or 112

For immediate medical emergencies

Warning Signs Requiring Immediate Help:

  • Thoughts of suicide or self-harm
  • Making plans to end your life
  • Feeling completely hopeless
  • Hearing voices or seeing things others don't
  • Believing others want to harm you

These are medical emergencies. Please reach out immediately.
