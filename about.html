From Personal Pain to Purpose

My name is Daniel Manyasa, and this is my story.

In 2018, I lost my grandmother—the woman who raised me, who taught me about resilience, who was my anchor in life's storms. Her passing left a void that nothing could fill. The grief was overwhelming, and I struggled to find my footing.

As I navigated my own grief, I began to notice something: I wasn't alone in my pain. I saw young men and women in my community struggling with addiction, trying to numb their own pain. I watched parents visiting the graves of their children—a pain no parent should ever know. I witnessed the silent suffering of those battling mental health issues alone.

One truth became clear: We don't hate people who struggle with addiction. We hate the addiction that has taken hold of them. We're all here to help each other.

STILL STANDING was born from this realization. It started as a simple idea: What if we created a space where no one had to suffer alone? What if we could provide the support I wish I had when I lost my granny?

Today, STILL STANDING is more than a website—it's a community, a sanctuary, a place where broken hearts find healing and lost souls find hope.

Your pain can become your purpose. Your struggle can become your strength. Your story can become someone else's survival guide.

— Daniel Manyasa

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To provide compassionate, accessible support for individuals navigating grief, addiction recovery, and mental health challenges through peer-led counseling, community connection, and evidence-based resources.

Our Vision

A world where no one suffers alone, where mental health support is stigma-free and accessible to all, and where every person struggling finds the strength to keep standing.

Our Promise

To walk alongside you in your darkest moments, to provide hope without judgment, and to create a safe space where healing can begin—one step, one day, one story at a time.

Our Journey

2018

The Beginning of Pain

Daniel loses his grandmother, sparking a personal journey through grief that would eventually lead to STILL STANDING.

2019

Finding Purpose

After witnessing others struggling alone with grief, addiction, and mental health, the idea for STILL STANDING begins to form.

2020

First Steps

Daniel begins informal peer counseling in his community, helping others navigate their struggles.

2021

Formal Training

Daniel completes certification as a peer counselor and begins developing the ARR (Addiction Recovery Rules) program.

2022

Community Grows

What started as individual support grows into a small community of mutual aid and support.

2023

STILL STANDING is Born

The website is conceived to reach more people and provide 24/7 access to support and resources.

2024

Launch & Growth

STILL STANDING launches online, providing grief support, addiction recovery resources, mental health tools, and community connection to all who need it.
