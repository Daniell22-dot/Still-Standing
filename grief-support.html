Grief & Loss Support

Honoring your pain, supporting your healing journey

Understanding Your Grief

Grief is Not Linear

There's no "right way" to grieve. Your journey may include anger, denial, bargaining, depression, and acceptance—often in no particular order.

There's No Timeline

Grief doesn't follow a schedule. Whether it's been weeks or years since your loss, your feelings are valid.

You're Not Alone

Many have walked this path before. Sharing your story can help lighten the burden.

Support for Specific Losses

Losing a Parent or Grandparent

Losing a parent or grandparent often feels like losing your foundation. These relationships shape who we are.

Coping Strategies:

  • Create a memory book or box with photos and mementos
  • Continue their traditions or start new ones in their honor
  • Write letters to them expressing what you wish you could say
  • Talk about them with family members who share your loss

Losing a Child

No parent should have to bury their child. This is perhaps the most profound loss one can experience.

Parent Support Groups:

We offer confidential support groups specifically for bereaved parents. These groups provide a safe space to share your pain with others who truly understand.

Losing a Partner or Spouse

The loss of a life partner affects every aspect of daily life and future plans.

Daily Coping Tools

01

Breathing Exercises

When grief feels overwhelming, focus on your breath. Try the 4-7-8 technique: Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7, exhale for 8.

02

Grief Journaling

Write letters to your loved one, record memories, or simply express your feelings. No one else needs to read it.

Download Journal Template
03

Memory Rituals

Light a candle on special dates, visit meaningful places, or cook their favorite meal. These rituals keep their memory alive.

When to Seek Immediate Help

If you're experiencing any of the following, please reach out immediately:

  • Thoughts of harming yourself or others
  • Inability to perform daily tasks for an extended period
  • Using alcohol or substances to cope with grief
  • Feeling completely detached from reality
